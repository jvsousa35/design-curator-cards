## Card 1 — Bauhaus (1919–1933)

### Defining traits (pin)
- Form follows function
- Integration of art, craft, and industry
- Geometric abstraction
- Minimal ornamentation
- Functional typography

### Key people / places
- Walter Gropius (founder)
- Paul Klee
- Wassily Kandinsky
- Dessau Bauhaus building

### Vocabulary bank (real design terms)
- Modernism
- Functionalism
- Geometric abstraction
- Sans-serif typography
- Industrial design
- Constructivism influence
- Rationalism
- Primary color theory

### Real examples to link later (2+)
- Bauhaus Dessau Foundation
- MoMA Bauhaus Collection

### Notes / angles
Bauhaus established a systematic, rational design language that would heavily influence modern typography and layout systems.


## Card 2 — Swiss Style / International Typographic Style (1950s–1970s)

### Defining traits (pin)
- Grid-based layout systems
- Asymmetrical composition
- Objective photography
- Clean sans-serif typography
- Mathematical precision

### Key people / places
- Josef Müller-Brockmann
- Armin Hofmann
- Emil Ruder
- Zurich School of Design

### Vocabulary bank
- International Typographic Style
- Grid system
- Helvetica
- Akzidenz-Grotesk
- Asymmetry
- Visual hierarchy
- Objective design
- Typographic discipline

### Real examples to link later (2+)
- Müller-Brockmann posters archive
- Museum für Gestaltung Zürich

### Notes / angles
Swiss Style formalized strict typographic systems emphasizing neutrality, clarity, and structure.

### Defining traits (pin)

- Broken grid systems
- Expressive letterforms
- Postmodern influence

### Key people / places
- Wolfgang Weingart
- April Greiman
- Dan Friedman
- Basel School influence

### Vocabulary bank
- Postmodern typography
- Typographic deconstruction
- Visual layering
- Expressive form
- Anti-grid movement
- Digital experimentation
- Conceptual typography

### Real examples to link later (2+)
- Weingart experimental typography archive
- April Greiman poster collection

### Notes / angles (how it reacts against Swiss)
